Content

Home page

My paid crochet patterns can be found on etsy at cottonwhipcrochet.etsy.com. The reason you will enjoy these patterns is they go into detail to help you achieve a similar result to my finished item. I specify the exact yarns used along with their color codes, and I provide plenty of photos in the pattern itself along with links to video tutorials. While I do not recommend these patterns for beginners, they are great for crocheters who know the basics of crochet like the single crochet (sc), the half double crochet (hdc) and the double crochet (dc). If you have made a few amigurumi in the past you will be able to follow along with these patterns. You can see some of my premium patterns here:

About Me

I started learning to crochet in 2013. My first project was a baby blanket, then I made an amigurumi turtle. I continued crocheting from then and learning new skills which pattern. In 2020 I was following along with a pattern to make a doll and I found myself making adjustments to certain parts. That is when I thought to myself, “I think I could design my own pattern”. I have been doing so since. After having spent years following other's patterns, I knew what I liked and didn't like. I loved detailed patterns that told me the exact yarns used, not just a category weight. I liked when they were well structured and easy to read. I loved it when they had photos or videos for the more complicated parts. All of these experiences lead me to polish my patterns to what they are currently. I'm always looking to improve and to grow as a crochet pattern designer. I hope you enjoy my patterns!

Free Patterns

I want to help you feel confident that my patterns are well written and detailed and also help out those who are trying out crochet and want to make something simpler. This page will hold free patterns and that you can follow to create your own amigurumi. LITTLE LAMB CROCHET PATTERN: YARNS: Finger weight (Category 1) Yarn I used Hobbii's Rainbow Cotton 8/4 and Hobbii's Boheme Velvet Fine Hobbii Raibow Coton 8/4 Cotton: • #89 Almond • #07 Brown Boheme Velvet Fine: • #17614 Snow White MATERIALS: • 1.75 mm and 2.50mm Crochet Hooks • 5mm black safety eyes • Threading needle • Stitch markers • Fiberfill for stuffing • Pins to place limbs temporarily • Scissors • Embroidery Thread in dark brown for mouth and ear details, and pink for nose. TERMS: • sl st= slip stitch • sk st= skip stitch • FL/FLO= Front loop/front loop only • BL/BLO= back loop/ back loop only • sc= single crochet • hdc= half double crochet • dc= double crochet • mc= magic circle • inc= increase • dec= decrease PATTERN: HEAD With Almond make a magic circle R1: sc 6 R2: inc x6 (12) R3: (sc, inc) x6 (18) R4: sc, inc, (sc 2, inc) x5, sc (24) R5: (sc 3, inc) x6 (30) R6: sc 2, inc, (sc 4, inc) x5, sc 2 (36) R7: (sc 5, inc) x6 (42) R8: sc 3, inc, (sc 6, inc) x5, sc 3 (48) R9-18: sc 48 (10 rounds) Eyes Attach your safety eyes now at R4 as shown. This will create a space of 8 rounds between each eye. Nose With pink embroidery thread, sew nose back and forth as follows, making your thread shorter in length as you move to the bottom. Now sew over the strands you made in a vertical direction. Start on the right as shown then go to the opposite end and work your way to the first vertical line you made. With dark brown embroidery thread sew mouth as shown. The vertical line coming down from the nose should be about one round long To find how long to make the smile, look to the farthest point of the nose on the right side and extend the smile that same length. Repeat for the left side. R19: sc 3, dec, (sc 6, dec) x5, sc 3 (42) R20: (sc 5, dec) x6 (36) R21: sc 2, dec, (sc 4, dec) x5, sc 2 (30) R22: (sc 3, dec) x6 (24) R23: sc, dec, (sc 2, dec) x5, sc (18) *Begin stuffing the head with fiberfill. R24: (sc, dec) x6 (12) R25: (sc 4, dec) x2 (10) Cut yarn leaving a long enough tail for closing as follows: Thread your end through a needle. Weave end through the BL of each of the 10 sts remaining. Pull tight and watch the hole close. Weave end. WOOL With Snow White make a magic circle R1: sc 6 R2: inc x6 (12) R3: (sc, inc) x6 (18) R4: sc, inc, (sc 2, inc) x5, sc (24) R5: (sc 3, inc) x6 (30) R6: sc 2, inc, (sc 4, inc) x5, sc 2 (36) R7: (sc 5, inc) x6 (42) R8-17: sc 42 (10 rounds) Place the wool over the head as follows and continue with pattern R18: (sc 19, dec) x2 (40) R19: (sc 8, dec) x4 (36) R20: (sc 7, dec) x4 (32) R21: (sc 6, dec) x4 (28) Cut yarn and weave end. APPENDAGES Horns (make 2) 1.75mm With Brown make a mc R1: sc 6 in mc R2: inc x6 (12) R3: sc 12 Cut yarn long enough to use to sew on to the head. Using a dark brown embroidery thread and the 1.75mm hook surface sl st in a circular path as shown Ears (make 2) 2.50mm With Snow White make a mc R1: sc 6 R2: (sc, inc) x3 (9) R3-4: sc 9 (2 rounds) R5: (sc, dec) x3 (6) R6-7: sc 6 (2 rounds) Cut yarn long enough to sew on. Arms (make 2) 1.75mm With Almonds make a mc R1: sc 6 in mc R2-4: sc 6 (3 rounds) Cut yarn long enough to close as follows: Thread your end through a needle. Weave end through the BL of each of the 6 sts remaining. Pull tight and watch the hole close. Weave end. ASSEMBLY Place the horns around R15 of the Wool. Place the ears directly beneath. The arms are placed at R18 beneath the ears.
